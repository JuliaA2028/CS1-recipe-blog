Cuscuz doce

Description

A coconut treat. It’s soft, a little sweet, and perfect with butter or condensed milk on top.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cornmeal (flocão de milho)
  • ½ cup grated coconut
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ cup milk (or water)
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Mix cornmeal, coconut, sugar, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Add milk until the texture feels a bit moist.
  3. Let it sit for 10 minutes to absorb.
  4. Steam for about 10–15 minutes, until firm and fluffy.
  5. Serve with a drizzle of condensed milk.

Quindim

Description

Quindim is a Brazilian classic made with egg yolks and coconut. It’s like flan but with a chewy coconut bottom.

Ingredients

  • 10 egg yolks
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 2 tbsp melted butter

Steps

  1. Mix egg yolks, sugar, coconut, and melted butter in a bowl.
  2. Grease small molds with butter and sprinkle with sugar.
  3. Pour the mixture into the molds.
  4. Bake in a water bath at 350°F (180°C) for 30–40 minutes.
  5. Let cool, then unmold carefully.

Brigadeiro

Description

Brazil’s favorite party treat! It’s like a chocolate truffle and it’s covered in sprinkles. Super easy.

Ingredients

  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Chocolate sprinkles (for rolling)

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, mix condensed milk, cocoa powder, and butter.
  2. Cook on medium heat, stirring constantly, until thick and pulling from the sides.
  3. Let it cool.
  4. Roll into small balls and coat with chocolate sprinkles.
